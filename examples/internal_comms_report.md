## Stage 1: Draft

**Subject: Q1 2026 All-Hands Update: Growth, Research Progress, and Path Forward**

**From:** Dario Amodei  
**To:** All Anthropic

---

## Opening

Team,

Q1 2026 delivered meaningful progress across product, research, and safety — while also highlighting areas where we need to improve our execution. I want to share our wins transparently alongside the challenges we're navigating.

## Product Momentum

**Claude Enterprise crossed 500 customers in Q1**, representing 40% quarter-over-quarter growth. This milestone reflects years of foundational work paying off: our safety-first approach is resonating with enterprises who need AI they can trust with their most sensitive work.

Customer feedback continues to drive our roadmap. The enterprise teams are seeing strong renewal rates and expansion within existing accounts, which tells us we're solving real problems.

## Research and Safety Leadership

**We published Responsible Scaling Policy v3** with concrete safety benchmarks that establish clearer thresholds for capability assessment. This represents months of collaboration between our safety, research, and policy teams. Other labs are already adapting similar frameworks — exactly the industry leadership we aim for.

**Three papers were accepted at ICML 2026**, including breakthrough work on constitutional AI and interpretability methods. Our research continues to advance both capability and safety in parallel, which remains core to who we are.

## Scaling Challenges

**Our headcount grew 30% in Q1** as we brought incredible talent across engineering, research, and go-to-market functions. This growth is necessary to execute on our opportunity, but it's straining our systems.

Honestly, our onboarding experience needs improvement. New team members are telling us it takes too long to become productive, and our internal tools haven't scaled with our growth. We're investing in dedicated onboarding infrastructure and streamlining access provisioning. Expect improvements by mid-Q2.

## Regulatory Environment

**The FTC inquiry continues**, and we're cooperating fully with their requests. Our legal team is handling this professionally, and we're confident in our practices and compliance posture. These processes take time, and I'll update you as appropriate while respecting the ongoing nature of the inquiry.

This regulatory attention comes with being a leader in AI. We've built Anthropic with transparency and responsibility from day one, which positions us well for increased scrutiny.

## Looking Ahead

Q2 priorities are clear: execute on enterprise growth, advance our research agenda, and build the internal infrastructure to support our scale. We're solving hard problems at the frontier of AI safety and capability — exactly what we set out to do.

Questions? Our next all-hands Q&A is April 15th. Leadership team office hours continue every Friday.

Thanks for everything you're building,

Dario

---

*This message will be archived in our internal wiki. Please direct questions about the FTC matter to legal@anthropic.com.*

## Stage 2: Editorial Review

**Tone:** 8/10
**Clarity:** 9/10
**Alignment:** 10/10
**Recommendation:** APPROVE

> The message strikes an excellent balance between optimism and transparency, covering all required points with appropriate sensitivity. The tone is professional yet authentic, and the structure makes it easy to scan. While there are minor areas for improvement around the FTC language, the overall message is ready for distribution.

### Sensitivity Flags

- Our legal team is handling this professionally, and we're confident in our practices and compliance posture
- These processes take time, and I'll update you as appropriate while respecting the ongoing nature of the inquiry

### Suggested Edits

- Consider softening 'we're confident in our practices and compliance posture' to 'we believe in our practices and compliance approach' to avoid appearing overconfident during an active investigation
- Add a brief acknowledgment that rapid growth can be challenging for existing employees, not just new hires, to show awareness of broader team impact
- Consider adding one concrete example of the onboarding improvements being implemented to make the commitment more tangible
